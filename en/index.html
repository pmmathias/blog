Notes from Conversations with a Machine

KI-Mathias

By day, I build software. I run a company, lead projects, make decisions under uncertainty. In the evenings, I ask questions. Not the kind you answer with a Google search – the kind that require three disciplines at once and still leave you unsure whether the answer is right.

Since large language models exist, I finally have a conversation partner for these questions. Claude Code by Anthropic – an AI patient enough not to give up after the fifteenth “But why?”, and broadly educated enough to assemble the answer from physics, mathematics, and philosophy at the same time.

This is the result. No textbooks, no lectures – conversations. Between a human who understands how to build complex systems, and a machine that understands how complex systems work. What comes out are texts, interactive visualizations, and thought experiments that go deeper than most popular science dares – and remain more accessible than most textbooks manage.

What drives me in this reminds me of Hermann Hesse’s Glass Bead Game: the idea that all sciences are ultimately variations of the same theme. That Fourier decompositions and music say the same thing. That eigenvalues simultaneously explain quantum mechanics, artificial intelligence, and the structure of the universe. That emergence – the arising of something greater from something smaller – is the most fundamental process of all: in physics, in consciousness, in language models, and perhaps even in what people once called “God.”

But unlike Hesse, there is no authoritarian institution here that decides what the Glass Bead Game is and who gets to play. Everyone can play now. A language model and a curious human are enough. This is the proof.

We are living in the years when large language models are beginning to change the way people think, learn, and create. Not as a replacement for human thought – but as an amplifier. A catalyst. The smart friend you call at three in the morning because you just understood why \(e^{iS/\hbar}\) is the only possible form of the quantum amplitude – and you need to tell someone.

This blog is my archive of those conversations. An archive of sparks.
